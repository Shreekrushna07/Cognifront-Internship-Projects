Sinhagad is an ancient hill fortress located at around 49 km southwest of the city of Pune, India. It was previously known as Kondhana and has been the site of many battles, especially the Battle of Sinhagad in 1670, where the Maratha warrior Tanaji Malusare sacrificed his life to capture the fort from the Mughals. The fort is now a popular tourist attraction and a trekking destination, offering scenic views of the Sahyadri Mountains and the nearby Khadakwasla Dam

Some of the attractions of the fort are:

  • The Kaundinyeshwar temple, which is believed to be built around 2000 years ago by the sage Kaundinya
  • The memorial of Tanaji Malusare, which is a stone structure with a metal bust of the hero and a plaque describing his valour
  • The Kalyan Darwaja and Pune Darwaja, which are the two main gates of the fort. The Kalyan Darwaja is towards the southeast and the Pune Darwaja is towards the northeast
  • The Sinhagad Fort Museum, which displays weapons, armour, coins, pottery, paintings and other artefacts related to the history of the fort

The fort is open from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM and the entry fee is Rs. 25 per person2 You can also enjoy the local cuisine of the fort, such as bhakri, pithale, vangyache bharit, matki usal, thecha, kanda bhaji, dahi and tak.

In 1636, the Adil Shahi sultanate of Bijapur invaded the kingdoms to its south.[6] The sultanate had recently become a tributary state of the Mughal empire.[6][31] It was being helped by Shahaji, who at the time was a chieftain in the Maratha uplands of western India. Shahaji was looking for opportunities of rewards of jagir land in the conquered territories, the taxes on which he could collect as an annuity.

Shahaji was a rebel from brief Mughal service. Shahaji's campaigns against the Mughals, supported by the Bijapur government, were generally unsuccessful. He was constantly pursued by the Mughal army, and Shivaji and his mother Jijabai had to move from fort to fort.
